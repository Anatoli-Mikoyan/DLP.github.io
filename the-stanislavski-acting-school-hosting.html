Hostings

In Minsk, trainees are accommodated in typical Belarusian flats. Accommodation generally sleeps between four and six people.
They share simple but comfortable rooms with two, three or four beds. Single rooms can be booked on request (see single room rates). The accommodation is fully equipped. Participants live in a community and are all autonomous and responsible for the smooth running of the centre. All day-to-day tasks are shared equally. The flats are located in the city centre of Minsk. They are therefore well served by public transport, particularly the metro and tram.
This means that participants can easily and quickly reach the various training venues as well as the city's landmarks. Living in the heart of Belarus gives trainees a chance to discover the country's cultural activities more fully, and provides an opportunity for exchanges around artistic creation. This unique setting for creative immersion also offers participants a deeper involvement in Belarusian life and community, making for an intense and unique theatrical experience.



In Belarus, to be registered for the year, you must be registered alone in one room, but you can have several people in the same flat, as long as there are rooms in it. Over the last few years, we have noticed that flat prices have changed relatively depending on the district and the condition of the flat, whether it is furnished in the style of the Soviet period or whether it is brand new, and also whether you want a flat for yourself or whether you are prepared to share it with 2 or 3 people or live in a room in a family home. Prices for 3-bedroom flats can range from €250 to €750, and homestay accommodation is the cheapest. Depending on your choices, you can stay for less than 100 euros, but it will be very basic, and more than 250 euros and it will be very comfortable, perhaps even too comfortable...
